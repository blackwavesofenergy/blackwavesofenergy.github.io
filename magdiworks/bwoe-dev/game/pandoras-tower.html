pandora’s tower, and the ‘look at your girlfriend’ button

Any JRPG fanatic must be familiar with ‘Operation Rainfall’; a rare petition to localise three Wii RPGs. The ringleader was ‘Xenoblade Chronicles’, which was already released in Europe, as well as Mistwalker’s ‘The Last Story’, directed by former Final Fantasy head Hironobu Sakaguchi, and today’s topic, the eclectic ‘Pandora’s Tower’. That, unfortunately, seems to be its legacy - a footnote of a successful campaign. But what if I told you that ‘Pandora’s Tower’ is not just a fantastic game, but perhaps the best of the three? Developed by Ganbarion in conjunction with Nintendo, ‘Pandora’s Tower’ is a product of Nintendo desperately trying to win over the hardcore audience with mature second-party titles, alongside the Japanese exclusive ‘Zengeki no Regnleiv’ (which can best be described as Earth Defence Force crossed with Skyward Sword crossed with Norse mythology) and ‘Fatal Frame: Mask of the Lunar Eclipse’. Pandora’s Tower’s setting straddles the line between JRPG extravagance and being unique.
